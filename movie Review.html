storyline:

A middle-aged school teacher, Gandhi Mahaan, embarks on a journey of self-discovery after getting abandoned by his family. He soon becomes a pride billionaire but faces many challenges.

Review:

Mahaan is a solid, very well-written, slow-paced, stylishly shot, and neatly executed Gangster Drama. A typical Karthik Subbaraj film with the gangster setup, well-defined characters, plot twists, and retro cars. With a familiar setup, he has corrected the things which went wrong in (Jagame Thandhiram), which is energy in the screenplay. Unlike that film, this film has infectious energy and liveliness in the screenplay which helped keep things engaging throughout the 162 minutes. A pretty well-established first half followed by a decent second half. There are some pacing issues with the film that could easily trim 15-20 minutes from its runtime. The 3rd act twist is predictable but nonetheless is anchored effortlessly by Vikram. That single-take action sequence by him blew my mind away and reminded me of (Kingsman's - The Church Fight sequence). Dhruv Vikram is quite good for his age, superb effort. The father-son confrontation and the mind game in the climax is a scream! All in All, Mahaan is a thoroughly enjoyable masala entertainer which has all the elements to keep you invested in its runtime. Father and son going against each other put up a terrific show that shouldn't be missed. DO WATCH IT.
